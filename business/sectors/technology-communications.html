What This Sector Is

Technology and communications in Greenland encompass internet connectivity, mobile networks, satellite services, and the physical infrastructure required to support reliable communication across long distances and remote terrain.

Connectivity is not solely a consumer service; it underpins aviation, maritime operations, emergency response, scientific research, and broader commercial activity.

Why It Matters Now

Communications infrastructure is increasingly treated as strategic, supporting security coordination, logistics, and economic growth. As Arctic activity increases, connectivity and coverage can become limiting factors for both public services and private investment.

This sector also intersects with broader questions around resilience, redundancy, and international technology partnerships.

Current State

Connectivity varies significantly by region and settlement size. Remote conditions and long distances increase infrastructure cost and maintenance complexity, resulting in uneven service quality and limited redundancy in some areas.

Improvements typically occur through phased network upgrades, satellite capacity changes, or targeted infrastructure projects rather than abrupt transitions.

What to Watch

  • Satellite capacity expansions and new service agreements
  • Network upgrades, coverage initiatives, or new routes
  • Resilience, redundancy, and backup connectivity projects
  • International partnerships affecting infrastructure deployment
