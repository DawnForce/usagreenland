Greenland’s mineral wealth has long been acknowledged, but recent geopolitical shifts have intensified global interest in its rare earth and critical mineral deposits.

These resources are essential for advanced manufacturing, renewable energy systems, and defense technologies — placing Greenland at the center of supply chain diversification efforts.

“Control of critical minerals is increasingly seen as a national security issue.”

Despite growing attention, extraction faces significant constraints. Environmental protections, limited infrastructure, and local opposition to large-scale mining projects have slowed development.

Greenlandic authorities have emphasized that any mineral partnerships must align with long-term sustainability goals. This has narrowed the field of potential investors and increased scrutiny of foreign involvement.

As competition for resources intensifies globally, Greenland’s approach highlights the tension between strategic demand and local governance — a balance that will shape future negotiations.
