Greenlandic leaders have responded cautiously to renewed U.S. proposals, emphasizing that any future arrangement must respect Greenland’s autonomy and existing constitutional ties with Denmark.

Officials in Nuuk have reiterated that Greenland is open to cooperation — particularly in areas such as infrastructure investment and Arctic research — but reject narratives suggesting a loss of self-governance.

“Greenland is not for sale. Cooperation must be built on partnership and respect.”

Danish officials echoed this position, framing the discussions as part of long-standing defense and security coordination under NATO, rather than bilateral negotiations over territory.

Analysts note that Greenland’s response is carefully calibrated: welcoming economic engagement while signaling firm political boundaries. This posture aims to preserve leverage as interest from multiple global powers increases.
